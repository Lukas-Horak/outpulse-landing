# Platform — Outbound Campaign Brief: Visionary Victor

## Campaign Goal

Sell the productized service **"Full Product in 30 Days"** ($15K–$25K) to funded startup founders who need a working prototype or MVP to raise their next round, impress investors, or launch to market.

**Positioning:** This is not a dev shop pitch. This is a founder with 20 years in product, award-winning design work, and a multi-million-revenue studio personally leading a 30-day sprint. That's the differentiator.

---

## Target Persona: Visionary Victor

Funded startup founder or co-founder (seed to Series A) with a validated idea but no product team or a very small team that can't ship fast enough. They have capital, urgency, and are actively looking for a way to go from concept to working product without hiring a full team.

**Why this persona first:** Shortest sales cycle of all four personas. They have budget (funding), urgency (runway is burning), and a clear buying trigger (need product to raise next round / launch). Decision maker = buyer — no procurement, no committee.

## Email Sequence (3 steps)

**Sending schedule:**
- Email 1: Day 0 (cold open — about THEIR problem)
- Email 2: Day 3 (credibility — why YOU are different)
- Email 3: Day 7 (final — scarcity + graceful close)

**Tone:** Founder-to-founder. Direct, no fluff. Short paragraphs. No corporate speak.

**Strategy:** Email 1 hooks with their pain + shows the work (portfolio). Email 2 builds personal credibility + scarcity. Email 3 closes gracefully with social proof. Every email has portfolio link as proof and booking link as CTA. The P.S. line on Email 1 is a meta-proof: the email itself was sent from a product we built.

---

### Email 1 — Cold Open (Day 0)

**Subject line options (A/B test):**
- A: `{{company}} — from idea to product in 30 days`
- B: `quick question about {{company}}`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Saw that {{company}} recently raised funding — congrats. The hard part starts now: you need a product that matches the vision you sold investors.

I run a product studio called Platform. We take funded startups from concept to fully working product in 30 days — design, frontend, backend, infrastructure. Not wireframes. Not mockups. A real product your users can touch.

Here's what that looks like: https://plat4m.com/work

We've shipped 100+ products across fintech, healthtech, SaaS, and e-commerce. Pricing starts at $15K for a full sprint.

If this fits what you're building, I'd love a 30-minute call:
{{booking_link}}

Lukas

P.S. Even this email was sent from a product we designed and built ourselves in under a month — Outpulse (outpulse.ai). That's the kind of speed we bring to every project.
```

**Why this works:**
- Opens with their win (funding) → names their real anxiety (build what you pitched)
- Portfolio link is proof, not a CTA — "here's what that looks like" lets them verify quality
- Booking link is the single clear action
- P.S. line is meta-proof: the email IS the demo. Founders will click Outpulse out of curiosity
- Two links but different roles: portfolio = evidence, booking = action

### Email 2 — Personal Credibility + Scarcity (Day 3)

**Subject:** `re: {{company}} — from idea to product in 30 days`

**Body:**

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Quick context on why I reached out personally — I don't run an agency that assigns you a random team.

I've been designing and building digital products for 20 years. My studio has delivered for Spotify, Capital Group, and Gartner. I've built my own company to seven-figure revenue. When I say "full product in 30 days," I mean I personally lead every sprint.

You can see the caliber of work here: https://plat4m.com/work

That's also why I only take on 2–3 projects at a time. I have one slot open for May.

If {{company}} needs a product that actually ships — not just looks good in Figma — let's talk:
{{booking_link}}

Lukas
```

**Why this works:**
- "Why I reached out personally" — instantly differentiates from mass outreach
- Three proof points in three sentences: 20 years, blue-chip clients, own business success
- Portfolio link reinforces credibility — after reading about you, they want to see the work
- "2–3 projects at a time" — natural, believable scarcity
- "One slot open for May" — specific, real urgency

---

### Email 3 — Final + Graceful Close (Day 7)

**Subject:** `closing the loop`

**Body:**

```
{{first_name}} — last note from me.

If building {{company}} fast isn't a priority right now, totally get it.

But if you're sitting on a funded idea and need it shipped before your runway gets shorter — this is what I do. 30 days. $15K–$25K. A product your investors and users can actually use.

One of my recent founders closed their bridge round 2 weeks after we shipped their MVP. That's the kind of outcome I optimize for.

See what we've built: https://plat4m.com/work
Book a call: {{booking_link}}

Either way — good luck with {{company}}.

Lukas
```

**Why this works:**
- Graceful exit — no begging, no "just checking in"
- Restates the full offer one final time (price, timeline, outcome)
- Social proof one-liner: bridge round closed after we shipped
- Both links on separate lines — clean, scannable, last chance to click either
- "Good luck" — genuine, human, leaves the door open

## A/B Testing Plan

**Week 1–2:** Subject line test. Split 50/50:
- A: `{{company}} — from idea to product in 30 days`
- B: `quick question about {{company}}`

**Week 3–4:** Winner → test Email 1 body variants:
- A: Lead with funding congratulation (current version)
- B: Lead with pain: *"Hiring a dev team takes 3 months. You don't have 3 months."*

**Week 5+:** Scale winning combo. Add new geographies. Test adding pricing directly in subject line: `$15K to ship {{company}} in 30 days`

## Scarcity Model

The "2–3 projects at a time" and "one slot open" messaging is only credible if it's real. Recommendations:

- Update the slot count in Email 2 every 2–4 weeks based on actual availability
- If fully booked, switch to: *"Next available slot is July"* — this actually increases perceived value
- Never say "limited spots" generically — always give a specific number or month
- If a prospect replies late, you can use: *"I had a cancellation — one slot just opened for June. Interested?"*
