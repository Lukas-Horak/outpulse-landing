Brand Foundations

From cold outreach
to warm meetings.

Our colour system is a narrative, not a palette. On the left we start cold — the deep blues of the first unopened message, of a stranger's inbox, of prospects that don't know we exist yet. On the right we arrive warm — the oranges and peaches of a booked meeting, a handshake, a closed deal. Every gradient you see in the product walks that path: from cold to warm, from unknown to customer.

Cold · outreach · unknown Warm · meeting · customer
Identity

01The mark.

Two circles, overlapping — one hollow, one filled. The left ring is the prospect, outlined and empty; the right disc is the customer, filled and committed. The thin white crescent between them is the handshake: the precise moment outreach becomes a relationship.

Narrative

02Cold left, warm right.

Every brand surface reads from cold to warm. A hero gradient, a chart, a CTA section — all of them move the eye from blue (prospect in stranger territory) to orange (prospect who said yes). Hold this story straight and the product writes itself visually.

01 — Cold

Unread inbox, unknown person, unopened possibility.

Deep blues communicate distance, unfamiliarity, the "before" state.

02 — Warm

Oranges and peaches communicate trust, motion, human warmth.

04The spectrum stack.

Typography

Buttons

06Pill-shaped, fully rounded.

Every CTA is a pill — no soft-corner rectangles. The orange primary is the only high-intensity call; everything else supports. Use at most one orange button per view.

07Status, signal, label.

08Spacing & radius.

Spacing scales on an 8-point grid with a handful of named steps. Radius stops hit at 8 (chips), 12 (cards), 16–20 (browser mockups), 32 (CTA cards), 999px (pills). Everything else is a bug.

Pattern

09Cold-to-warm composition.

A ready-to-use split composition. Cold side carries the "before" — stranger, inbox, silence. Warm side carries the "after" — reply, meeting, deal.

Before
"Who even are they?"

A cold name in a list of 275M. Zero context, zero warmth, zero reason to reply.

After
"Let's hop on a call."

Researched, personalized, relevant. The inbox is warm and the meeting is on the calendar.
